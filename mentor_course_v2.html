The 8 pillars
Design principles
Practice over theory. Every session: 10 min concept, 30 min doing, 5 min reflection. Students never just listen.
Islam is the spine. Not a separate subject — values like modesty, gratitude, discipline, and care for others are woven into every pillar.
Phone-first. Every activity works on an Android phone with an AI app. No laptop, no peer needed.
Solo but not lonely. Activities are designed for one person — journaling, AI conversations, voice recordings, observations.
Rituals compound. A 5-min daily ritual shared across sessions builds the habits that outlast the course.
Morning ritual (5 min)
1
Bismillah intention
Set one intention for the day out loud or in a voice note. Connects Islamic practice to daily action.
2
One sentence journal
Type one sentence in their notes app: "Today I want to feel ___." Builds self-awareness over weeks.
Evening ritual (5 min)
3
3 wins + 1 struggle
Voice note or written. Three things that went well, one thing that was hard. No fixing, just noticing.
4
One Alhamdulillah
Name one specific thing to be grateful for. Not generic — must be something from today. Trains the gratitude muscle.
Weekly ritual (10 min, on session day)
5
Weekly reflection question
Each session ends with one question they answer in writing and share with the mentor. Builds accountability without pressure.
6
Skill proof
One tangible output per session — a voice recording, a written paragraph, a screenshot, a drawing. Evidence of learning.
Why rituals matter
The rituals are more important than the sessions. A student who misses a session but keeps the morning ritual is still growing. Introduce all rituals in week 1. Revisit and celebrate them every 4 weeks.
